Cirque du Soleil: A mesmerizing piece that gives joy in everyone's eyes!

A groundbreaking entertaining piece brought together by not one production, but three, Cirque du Soleil!

By Archanan Atputharajah
Published February 23rd @ 2:56pm

KURIOS - Cabinet of Curiosities

Cirque du Soleil was a very interesting piece, with very few words spoken and a lot of action. The actions spoke the words, which was enough for the audience to make out what was really happening on stage. The expressions and actions were very well done and clear. The stunts were very entertaining and mesmerizing to watch, flipping through objects, high jumps, etc. The lighting, colours, and props used throughout the play were very beautiful and eye-catching. However, the play was a bit confusing for me at the beginning, it really didn't make sense or have a beginning, middle or end. The show was very interesting to watch, but most events throughout the play had me puzzled.

The audience sitting in front watching as the play goes on seemed very mind-blown by the stunts that they managed to pull. Just imagine how long they must've practiced for this. During the cat scene, many of the viewers were entertained and laughing by the expressions he gave. Who knew an individual can make someone laugh without any noise? The play also had the audience participate in the cat scene, which was very surprising and not expected. Deep inside, I believe the audience stayed entertained and mostly understood what was going on. They seemed very interested in the stunts and at the end of the performance, the claps given weren't just ordinary claps given for a presentation, it was more than just a play.

This show was an excellent success and really spoke comedic language using only expressions. In particular, during KURIOS - Cabinet of Curiosities, I found not only myself but the whole crowd laughing at the brilliant act portrayed by Facundo Gimenez. His actions have really portrayed what he was trying to become, a cat. The movements, facial expressions, and sounds were all on point and really conveyed the importance of drama. This specific scene was very clear to me and I understood what exactly was going on. The protagonist was walking home from a rainy day, and when he reaches home, he stumbles upon a woman who knocks on his door. He has fallen in love with the beauty of the woman and greets her into the house. She sits beside him on a couch, and the actor tries to put his hand on her but sadly fails. He goes off to the kitchen to get drinks for the two, and the actor then transforms into a cat and plays his role as a cat. The cat makes his way to the couch where the girl was sitting and entertains the audience with the expressions given by the actor. The cat then leaves the scene and the man returns with the drinks.

Overall, this play was wonderfully done and I really hope to see much more plays done by Cirque du Soleil. Rating: 5/5
