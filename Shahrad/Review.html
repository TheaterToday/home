Cirque du Soleil: organization in disorganization

By Shahrad Aslani
Published February 27th @ 11:21pm

Part of 'O'

The audience watches in awe as performers jump off ladder and onto a net below, doing acrobatics midair without any fears. Cirque du Soleil has truly outdone themselves with their 3 performances; “Cabinet of Curiosities”, “O” and “LUZIA”.

The performers maintain a gleeful and happy tone and demeanor throughout the first performance while doing difficult and potentially dangerous acrobatics, all whilst maintaining calm composure. This allowed the performance to contain an extra layer of dazzle and had me and the audience mesmerized and laughing at the performance.

The music fit the actions and the actions shown on stage which absorbed the audience more into the story. The usage of lighting highlighted parts of the performance and the focus of the stage at that moment in time, when the different acts started and ended and changing tone and emotions, such as blue light to convey sadness and yellow for energeticness or happiness.. The absence of music also allowed the performers to make sounds and not distract from the act. It almost couldn't be distinguished whether the actors, mainly the cat in the first performance, were making sounds or if they were outside sound effects. The seamless transition to the next performance was funny as well as engaging the viewers in the environment.

The second performance, “O” (pronounced as “eau”, meaning water) starts out on a water covered stage after a dramatic scene. Keeping the center of the stage was beneficial to focusing the audiences' attention to the acrobatics happening in the floating center stage. The change of music and lighting to a made the transition in plot very clear and precise, while maintaining its fluidity. The outfits helped separate the performers who seemed almost identical, while still helping them fit in with their environment and the lighting. The set being mostly water intrigued me; how they used such a linear, basic environment in such a unique way was beyond belief. The use of lighting as a background was extraordinary and out of the box, an outstanding idea and very well executed. The costumes added to the performance by showing what the audience should focus on, and adding to the environment with the costumes, especially the fish scales.

The next performance, another seamless transition from the last, while remaining serious, provides a bit of humor when the performer lights another's shoes on fire as he sits there, completely oblivious to the world around him as he slowly burns. This normally dangerous action is handled professionally by the performer and the technical team behind the performance, making his clothing and chair burn for so long without harming the actor. Stunts are why Cirque du Soleil is held in such high regard in the world of theater. The many hazardous things going on in this environment had the audience and I in awe, applauding. The prop animal blended in really well with the background and fooled the audience and I into almost believing it was a live animal. This performance was spectacular.

My final rating is 8/10. It could potentially have more complex and dramatic story lines, however, it would be difficult to express in an entertaining theatrical manner and would add to the confusion the play already contains. I would definitely recommend this performance.
